          UWE Bristol SuperCollider Workshop

          This workshop will focus on the SuperCollider programming language and its powerful sound synthesis engine and interactive features. I will introduce some basic SC concepts to get us started. Then, we will examine the possibilities of the language for creative coding, with particular emphasis on live coding. I will also critically examine some existing live coding practices and propose solutions to certain problems that arise from live coding with human musicians playing 'real' instruments in improvised settings. I will briefly explain my own live coding environment which prioritises modularity, immediacy and exchange in improvisation. To save time, I will ask you to please install the latest version of SuperCollider in your computer before our session. You can download it by following this link: http://supercollider.github.io/download. You can also download the sc3-plugins (providing additional synthesis, analysis, and other features) here: https://github.com/supercollider/sc3-plugins/releases.

          Short Bio

          Federico Reuben is a composer, live electronics performer, sound artist and academic. He writes music for humans and computers; writes about music and its intersection with technology, science and culture; makes sound art installations and performances; and is active as a live coding laptop improviser. Currently, he is Senior Lecturer in Music Technology at the University of York.
